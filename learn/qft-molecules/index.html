Concept and scope

What does quantum field theory add to a molecule?

The app deliberately narrates familiar quantum chemistry in field language. That viewpoint is valid and useful, but it must not be confused with an explicit ab initio quantum-electrodynamical calculation.

Open the QFT and cavity views

Particles are quanta of fields

In quantum field theory the electron field fills space, and an electron is one quantum of that field. A molecular state is a bound many-body state with a fixed number of electron-field excitations interacting with nuclei and the electromagnetic field.

In the nonrelativistic, fixed-particle-number regime, second-quantized field notation and ordinary many-electron quantum mechanics describe the same electronic problem. Molecular orbitals are one-particle modes used to expand the field operator; occupying a mode means placing field quanta in it.

Density and exchange in field language

The displayed electron density is the expectation value ⟨ψ̂†(r)ψ̂(r)⟩. Fermionic field operators anticommute, and that algebra produces the antisymmetric Slater determinant, Pauli exclusion and the Hartree–Fock exchange term K.

The app's Coulomb attraction and repulsion are evaluated through the standard instantaneous 1/r interaction. In QED this interaction can be understood as the static Coulomb-gauge limit associated with virtual-photon exchange. The diagram is a conceptual translation; the code does not propagate photon modes during the HF calculation.

What the main engine actually solves

The numerical core is nonrelativistic Born–Oppenheimer quantum chemistry: clamped nuclei, Gaussian orbitals and RHF/UHF self-consistency. For two electrons, full CI treats electronic correlation exactly within the finite basis. Density maps, orbital levels and molecular properties all follow from that electronic calculation.

Not included.

The main engine does not calculate Lamb shifts, vacuum polarization, relativistic corrections or electron–photon correlated wavefunctions. Preset facts mentioning such effects are external scientific context.

Where molecular QED begins

Genuine cavity QED promotes selected electromagnetic modes to quantum degrees of freedom and couples them to matter, commonly through a Pauli–Fierz Hamiltonian. Depending on the approximation, the state then contains electronic, nuclear and photonic coordinates on equal footing.

Ab initio QED-HF, QED-DFT and QED coupled-cluster methods can predict how a cavity changes orbitals, energies and response. These are active research methods and are substantially more demanding than drawing an avoided crossing.

The cavity panel is a toy model

The panel uses a two-level mixing formula. It takes the Hartree–Fock HOMO–LUMO gap as a proxy excitation energy, combines it with a user-selected cavity energy and coupling, and plots lower and upper branches. At resonance the model gives a splitting controlled by the coupling.

A HF orbital gap is not generally an optical excitation energy. The panel omits transition dipoles from an excited-state method, self-polarization, many modes, dissipation and correlated electron–photon structure. Its purpose is to teach detuning, polarization and avoided crossings—not to predict polaritonic chemistry.

A scientifically safe workflow

  1. Use the main app to understand the bare molecule at HF level.
  2. Use the cavity panel only for qualitative parameter intuition.
  3. Export the molecular geometry and generate a bridge template.
  4. Validate with a native QED-HF, QED-DFT, coupled-cluster or dynamics code before publication.

About this project

This is a free, open-source quantum chemistry laboratory that runs entirely in your browser: no installation, no server, no sign-up. It solves the electronic structure of real molecules with the Hartree-Fock method (restricted and unrestricted, STO-3G, 6-31G and 6-31G* basis sets) and, for two-electron systems, the exact full CI - all in plain JavaScript, in seconds.

You can explore the electron density, canonical and Boys-localized molecular orbitals, electrostatic potential maps with electric field arrows, the electron localization function (ELF), the density Laplacian, spin density, 3D isosurfaces, MO level diagrams, Mulliken charges, Mayer bond orders, dipole moments and Koopmans ionization potentials. The toolset includes BFGS geometry optimization, harmonic vibrational analysis with IR spectra, Morse bond curves with vibrational wavefunctions, a click-to-build molecule editor and export to .cube, PNG and JSON.

Built as a teaching and self-study companion for chemistry and physics students: 18 preset molecules from H2 to benzene, an in-app guide with a glossary, and an interface in English, Russian, German, Spanish and Chinese. The QFT framing - molecules as bound states of the electron field - is explained in the built-in wiki.
